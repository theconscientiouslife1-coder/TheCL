The Environment: Consumption, Conscientiousness, and the Breaking Point

We live in a world where we have become sanitised and at worse apathetic by all we see and hear, scrolling past the constant churn of global ills.

By [Author Name]

Published on [Date] · [X min read]

We live in a world where we have become sanitised and at worse apathetic by all we see and hear. TV screens, personal devices are constantly churning out the ills of the world, however unless we are experiencing the catastrophises for ourselves, we simply scroll past.

The Economic Experiment of Consumption

We are almost 50 years into the economic experiment of neo-liberalism, which sought to allow the individual to do what they so wanted to i.e. the freedom to consume as much as they wanted. For over four decades we have witnessed consumption on a scale never witnessed before. [i] The basis of this new way of thinking was that it is impossible to build planned societies on the premise that individuals only have a limited amount of knowledge on how the world operates therefore to attempt to plan would not provide a universal picture as to what a society should be.

This rationale has unfortunately led to the situation that where individuals are unaware of what their consume actions lead to it almost excusing for not thinking beyond the effects that one’s consumption has. But the consequences of our actions have led us to where we are at the breaking point of the environment. Unfettered consumption is in contactisation to a finite world.

Action and Sacrifice Required

This is not an alarmist post on the state of the environment, but this slow decent into the destruction of the multi facets of the environment, requires action across the various stakeholders in society and at it begins at the personal level. Yes, there are negative consequences to this alternative i.e. jobs, livelihoods will be lost. **FREEDOM** will be sacrificed; **liberty** will be restricted. These are not frivolous sacrifices & warrant serious conversations to plan accordingly and ensure everyone is brought along. However, the argument for the environment is too strong to deny/ignore, the risk we are placing on the mainstays of life i.e. food & water is fundamentally wrong.

The modern economy works on the basis of all our individual desires being fulfilled and then more are brought about to entice us, which propels this ship forward. Once we have attained a desire we move on to the next [SCHOPENHAUER]. However, there is a debt to be paid for the accomplishment of those desires and it is the environment that pays a heavy price from the materials to the manufacture to its disposal.

There have been many scenarios where we have lusted after an object only to find when we attained it, the feeling did not meet the expectations we had, almost anticlimactic. A problem for the those who see the degradation of the environment is that it is too much embroiled in the climate change/decarbonisation conversation. Our over consumption is depleting the earth and when disposed it is not organically broken down.
